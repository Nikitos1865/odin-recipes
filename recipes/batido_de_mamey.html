Batido de Mamey Recipe

It's pronounced maMEY...

Mamey might sound like a strange name for a fruit for people who don't have much experience with Caribbean cooking and horticulture, but it is actually a staple fruit with an important place in Cuban and Mexican cultures. This is a recipe for a refreshing summer smoothie (batido) made with this wonderfully pink-orange, flavorful fruit. Note: it might be hard to get your hands on a fresh mamey in the United States, where I live, but worry not! This recipe works very well with frozen mamey you might be able to find in your latin/Caribbean grocery store's frozen food isle. This cold, fruity treat can often be found in Cuban bakeries and cafe's around south Florida and North Jersey, to get an idea of how it should taste.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 2 cups of mamey pulp (frozen is fine!)
  • 2 cups of ice
  • A pinch of salt

Preparation/Steps

The preparation for this smoothie is simple as can be! Great for larger parties, since you can make a fairly large amount at a time.

  1. All you really need to do is put all the ingredients in blender, and blend for about 30-60 seconds until you reach your desired consistency. You should get a very creamy smoothy. If you find that it is too thick, you can add water to thin it out little by little.
  2. Add the ice after the other ingredients and blend for a few more minutes until completely smooth.
  3. Serve immediately!

    4. Note: This smoothie is better consumed immediately. After a few hours it can change its taste and even turn slightly bitter.
